Single Player Weapon Fusing Chart - [MC01]
==============================================

In single player mode you can fuse a weapon added to a command slot with an
attack spell in any previous command slot. 

========================================================
+  weapon  +  slot 1  +  slot 2  +  slot 3  +  slot 4  +
========================================================
|  F-SWORD |   fire   |  weapon  |          |          |
--------------------------------------------------------
|  B-SWORD | blizzard |  weapon  |          |          |
--------------------------------------------------------
|  T-SWORD |  thunder |  weapon  |          |          |
--------------------------------------------------------

MultiPlayer Timing Chart - [MC04]
==============================================

Instructions:
After The first player (whoever initiates the spell) releases their focused
spell, the following players must release their spells one after another within
the amount of time indicated in the charts.

ex: Fira+2 - Type A - everyone charges their Fire spells. Player 1 releases
their charge. Players 2 through 4 must all release their charges within .33
seconds of Player 1's release

ex: Firaga+2 - Type D - everyone charges their Fire spells. Player 1 releases
their charge. Player 2 must release their charge between .66 and 1.33 seconds
of Player 1's release. Player 3 must release their charge between 1.33 and 2
seconds of Player 1's release. Player 4 must release their charge between 2 and
2.66 seconds of Player 1's release.

Multi Player Timing Info - [MC05]
==============================================

The following was submitted by PerfectDar. The info is a bit unclear, but I'll
look into it and see if I can find a clearer version.


 Instead of counting seconds,  if you look closely, when you line 
up spell cursors, the crystals on the cursor rotate.  Instead of 
counting milliseconds - because it's too hard - count the rotations. 
 I'm not exactly sure how to translate my "rotation theory" into 
your seconds theory, but I can tell you that to cast thundaga + 
1, we had everyone delay for a full rotation of the crystals.  This 
means that the crystal on the top went down to the bottom position, 
then came back up to the top.  Other spells that needed short delays 
used a half rotation.  Of course, this method requires more coordination, 
but as long as everyone agrees that a rotation begins at the top 
and ends at the top, it is much more consistent then trial and error 
using time.
